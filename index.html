What is CFOP?

CFOP stands for:

  • Cross - Solve the white cross on the bottom
  • F2L - First Two Layers (solve corners and edges together)
  • OLL - Orient Last Layer (make the top face all yellow)
  • PLL - Permute Last Layer (solve the entire cube)

Basic Cross Strategy:

  1. Find a white edge piece
  2. Move it to the top layer
  3. Rotate the top to align the edge color with its center
  4. Flip it down with F2
  5. Repeat for all four edges

STEP 2: F2L (First Two Layers)

üéØ Goal: Solve corners and edges together as pairs

This step solves the first two layers by pairing corner and edge pieces and inserting them together.

Basic F2L Case: Corner and Edge in Top Layer

Scenario 1: Corner white facing up, edge on top
U R U' R'

Brings the pair together and inserts it

Scenario 2: Corner white facing side, edge on top
R U R'

Simple insertion when pieces are aligned

Scenario 3: Pair already formed in top layer
R U' R' U R U' R'

Direct insertion of formed pair

üß† Memory Trick: Think "Up, Right, Down, Left" - you're creating space, inserting the pair, and closing the space.
üí° Tip: Always keep the cross on the bottom. Rotate the entire cube to position your working slot in the front-right position.

STEP 4: PLL (Permute Last Layer) - FINAL STEP!

üéØ Goal: Solve the entire cube!

The top face is now all yellow. Now we need to move the pieces to their correct positions.

Sub-Step 1: Position the Corners

Look at the corner pieces: Find a corner that's already in the correct position (all three colors match the center colors of the three faces).

If you find one correct corner:

  1. Put that corner in the front-right position
  2. Use the algorithm below
  3. This will solve all four corners
U R U' L' U R' U' L
üß† Memory Trick: "Up-Right-Down, Left-back, Up-Right-back-Down, Left-forward"
Pattern: Move right side, move left side, opposite motions

If NO corners are correct:

Do the algorithm once from any angle, then you'll have one correct corner. Then follow the steps above.

üí° Important: After this step, the corners should all be in their correct positions (but edges might not be solved yet).

üÜò TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

Common Problems & Solutions

Problem: "My cross edges don't match the center colors!"

Solution: After getting white edges around the white center, you MUST rotate the top layer to align each edge color with its matching center BEFORE flipping it down with F2.

Problem: "I messed up during F2L!"

Solution: Take the pair out (reverse the insertion), reposition, and try again. If a corner or edge is in the wrong slot, move it to the top layer first.

Problem: "The yellow cross algorithm isn't working!"

Solution: Make sure you're holding the cube correctly:

  • DOT: any orientation
  • L-SHAPE: L must be in the top-left corner
  • LINE: must be horizontal (left to right)

Problem: "Sune isn't orienting all corners!"

Solution: You may need to do Sune multiple times (1-3 times). After each execution, check if all corners are yellow. If not, rotate the top layer and do Sune again.

Problem: "After PLL, my cube is still not solved!"

Solution: Make sure you're doing corners BEFORE edges. Also, check that you positioned the cube correctly before executing the algorithm (correct corner in front-right, or correct side in back).

Problem: "I can't remember all the algorithms!"

Solution: Don't try to memorize all at once! Focus on:

  1. F R U R' U' F' (yellow cross)
  2. R U R' U R U2 R' (Sune)
  3. U R U' L' U R' U' L (corners)
  4. One U-Perm for edges

These 4 algorithms can solve any cube! Learn others gradually.
